Gru-mobile: Autonomous Toy Development

Project Type: Freshman-Year BME Team Project

Project Overview

The Gru-mobile was a marketable children's toy designed as part of an introductory Biomedical Engineering team project. Our goal was to create an interactive, engaging, and functional toy that utilized sensor-based motion tracking.

Working in a team of five, we applied engineering design principles, consumer analysis, and embedded systems programming to develop a toy vehicle that autonomously followed objects using infrared and ultrasonic sensors.

Key Design & Engineering Aspects

  • Brainstorming & Consumer Analysis: Developed multiple toy concepts and refined the best option using survey data.

  • Project Presentation & Promotion: Created marketing materials and presented the final prototype to an audience.
